About

I am a Postdoctoral Scholar at Case Western Reserve University in the Lewin Lab. Broadly, I am interested in understanding how microbial diversity drives larger, ecosystem scale processes. I received my PhD from the UMass Amherst, where I was advised by Dr. Kristen M. DeAngelis. My dissertation research investigated the effects of microbial diversity on soil carbon formation and ecosystem function.

Research

  • Climate change effects on bacterial diversity
  • Bacterial strain level diversity in the vaginal microbiome
  • Microbial diversity and soil carbon formation
